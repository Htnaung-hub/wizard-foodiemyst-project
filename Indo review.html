    Cleanliness

    Hygiene in Indonesia is common in cities and tourist destinations, with government and certain policies trying to improve hygiene, leaving trash cans for street trash disposal, and taking care to enforce hygiene rules in restaurants.

    In Indonesia, hygiene is generally well-maintained in cities and tourist areas, but more attention should be given to waste management and littering issues in rural and densely populated regions.

    However, problems can still often be encountered outside of urban areas and in densely populated areas, such as littering and disposal of garbage on the runoff, and in some places poor proper collection can be detrimental to the sanitation of the natural environment.

    Healthiness

    There are health benefits to Indonesian vegetarian foods, as well as negative effects. The pros include:

    High in fish-based dishes such as mackerel, fried squid, and spicy fish, and rich in Omega-3 and protein, they are beneficial for cardiovascular and brain health.

    Fruits, fruits, and greens are rich in vitamins and fiber, which promote stomach function and immunity.

    The use of spices (turmeric, ginger, garlic, lemongrass) has boosted immunity and has antiviral and antibacterial properties.

    The downside is that there are plenty of fritters and fried foods that are traditionally high in fat, which can lead to high cholesterol → heart disease.

    Drinking sugary drinks (e.g., teh manis, es cendol, etc.) is high in sugar, → can lead to diabetes and diabetes.

    Soft drinks and soy sauce bases can lead to poor storage and high blood pressure.

    Preparation

    In Indonesian cuisines, you can find designs that add a variety of dishes, meat, fish, and beans to a single bowl (e.g., Nasi Campur, Nasi Padang). The dishes are decorated in natural colors such as red, green, yellow, etc., making them attractive to the diners. Some dishes are often used as the main flavors, such as ginger, graffiti, lemongrass, tea roots, etc., and are often used as special combination designs. It is beautifully presented with its leaf-shaped shape and aesthetic design.

    Indonesian cuisine deserves attention for its colorful presentation, natural ingredients, and unique use of spices that make each dish both attractive and flavorful.

    Special

    A unique feature of Indonesia is its rich cultural diversity reflected in colorful cuisine, traditional arts, and vibrant festivals.

    Nasi Goreng (Fried Rice): Commonly found at street food stalls, local markets, and hotels. Rendang (Spicy Beef Stew): Served at traditional homes, local eateries, and special events. Satay (Grilled Skewered Meat): Found at street vendors, festivals, and hotel buffets.
